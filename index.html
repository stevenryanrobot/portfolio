Wind-Robust Walking for Unitree G1 Humanoid Robot

Steven Yang. Course Project, Duke University. Feb 2026 – Mar 2026.

Designed and trained LSTM-PPO deep RL locomotion policies enabling a 35 kg, 12-DOF humanoid to maintain stable walking under sustained time-varying wind disturbances (up to 18 m/s, Beaufort 8), achieving 100% survival rate across all 84 test scenarios.

"The chart on the right is the most interesting finding — exp3 and exp5 both survive 100%, but exp3 actively leans into the wind while exp5 doesn't. PPO found an alternative: stepping instead of leaning. Reward shaping shapes how the robot survives, not whether it survives."

  • Three-layer physical wind model (base wind + OU turbulence + gusts) with per-rigid-body aerodynamic force computation
  • Six-stage curriculum learning controller, improving extreme wind survival rate from 48% to 100%
  • 15+ reward functions and 5 ablation studies (420 evaluations) to quantify component contributions
  • 4096 parallel GPU envs in Isaac Gym; sim-to-sim transfer in MuJoCo with multi-dim domain randomization

Reinforcement Learning · Isaac Gym · MuJoCo · PPO · LSTM · Python
